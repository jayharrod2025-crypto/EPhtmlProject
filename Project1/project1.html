Animal facts

  1. Certain species of wasp, known as parasitoids, inject their eggs into the eggs or bodies of other arthropods for them to grow, eventually killing the host
  2. Some parasitoids go after plants, laying eggs in their leaves and causing small bumps to form on the structure around the developing larvae
  3. Platypi, despite being mammals, have beaks (or something similar), lay eggs, and sweat milk instead of having set glands like other mammals
  4. Male platypi also have spurs in their hindlegs that inject extremely painful venom that can persist for months, and there is no antivenom
  5. Platypi are one of five species of monotreme, which are Earth's egg-laying mammals
  6. The other four species are all different species of echidna, the short-beaked, eastern long-beaked, western long-beaked, and Sir David's long-beaked, often called spiky anteaters due to their quills
  7. Some species of turtle that live in oceans or bodies of water that periodically freeze over can breathe through their rumps
  8. The animals that share the most similarly-coded genome to the hippopotamus, which means water-horse, are cetaceans like whales, as well as porcine animals like pigs
  9. Humans share more than %90 percent of their genome with apes from the pan genus, bonobos and chimpanzees
